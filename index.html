Who I Work With

Neurodivergent adults navigating identity, relationships, and the texture of daily life.

Neurodivergence

ADHD, Autism & Giftedness

Late-diagnosed and self-identified adults finding their way in a neurotypical world. We explore emotional intensity, rejection sensitivity, masking, executive function, and building a life that fits how your brain actually works.

OCD & Anxiety

Intrusive Thoughts & Compulsions

Harm OCD, relationship OCD, scrupulosity, contamination, "just right" experiences. I use Inference-Based CBT and Exposure & Response Prevention — evidence-based treatments that address OCD at its root.

Life & Identity

Grief, Transitions & Existential Questions

Divorce, loss, career shifts, identity confusion, existential anxiety, and the quiet suffering that doesn't always have a name. Therapy as a place to think and feel deeply about your life.

Relationships

Connection & Intimacy

Alexithymia, limerence, attachment patterns, and building relationships where you don't perform. I also work with partners and family members of neurodivergent adults.

Trauma

Relational & Developmental Trauma

Early experiences that shaped your nervous system and still show up in how you relate, regulate, and make meaning. Somatic and contemplative approaches alongside evidence-based modalities.

Identity & Culture

LGBTQ+, Multiracial & Cultural Identity

The intersections of neurodivergence, race, gender, sexuality, and spirituality. A practice that holds all of who you are without asking you to compartmentalize.

Therapeutic Approach

Presence, precision, and a deep respect for how your mind works.

Buddhist-Informed Practice

My approach to psychotherapy draws deeply from my Buddhist practice — a long-standing Vipassana practice under teacher Gil Fronsdal at Insight Meditation Center. This isn't an add-on; it's foundational to how I sit with people. I bring full presence to our work together, meeting suffering with curiosity and compassion rather than trying to fix or eliminate it.

Mindfulness isn't a technique I prescribe — it's the ground I practice from. This means I'm interested in what's actually happening for you, not in making you match a model of health. We explore your experience with precision and warmth.

Neurodiversity-Affirming

I integrate neurodiversity-affirming perspectives throughout all clinical work, whether we're focusing directly on neurodivergent experience or on relationships, grief, major transitions, or existential questions. Your neurotype shapes everything — how you process, relate, regulate, and make meaning.

My work isn't about fixing or normalizing you. It's about understanding who you are — and building a life that reflects that understanding. I work with the grain of your nervous system, not against it.

Evidence-Based Modalities

I only use treatments that have been proven to work, because I respect your time, energy, and money.

1
Inference-Based CBT (I-CBT)
A newer approach to OCD that targets the reasoning process behind obsessions, rather than just the anxiety they produce.
2
Exposure & Response Prevention (ERP)
The gold-standard treatment for OCD. Specialized training through the International OCD Foundation.
3
Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)
Skills for emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal effectiveness.
4
Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT)
Values-driven work that builds psychological flexibility and reduces the grip of avoidance.
5
Compassion-Focused Therapy
For people who are hard on themselves. Building a different relationship with your inner critic.
6
Somatic & Contemplative Approaches
Body-based awareness practices for trauma, nervous system regulation, and deepening self-knowledge.
7
Ketamine-Assisted Psychotherapy
Specialized training in therapeutic use of ketamine for treatment-resistant conditions.
8
Psychedelic Integration
Making meaning of psychedelic experiences within a clinical and contemplative framework.

Areas of Focus

Each area of focus reflects both clinical expertise and lived understanding.

Core Specialty

ADHD in Adults

Inattentive, combined, and late-diagnosed presentations. Executive function challenges, emotional dysregulation, time blindness, rejection sensitivity, and the grief that comes with late diagnosis. I understand ADHD from the inside out.

Core Specialty

OCD

Harm OCD, relationship OCD (ROCD), scrupulosity, intrusive thoughts, contamination, symmetry, and "just right" experiences. Treated with I-CBT and ERP — not talk therapy that accidentally reinforces the cycle.

Core Specialty

Autism in Adults

Late-diagnosed and self-identified adults. Masking, social exhaustion, sensory overwhelm, identity reconstruction, autistic burnout, and the intersection of autism with other neurodivergencies.

Specialty

Giftedness & Twice-Exceptional

The particular isolation and intensity of giftedness, especially when it co-occurs with ADHD, autism, or OCD. Overexcitabilities, asynchronous development, and the search for intellectual peers.

Specialty

Alexithymia

Difficulty identifying, naming, or describing emotions. Often co-occurs with autism and trauma. We build emotional vocabulary through somatic and experiential work, not by intellectualizing feelings.

Specialty

Limerence & Maladaptive Daydreaming

Intense, involuntary romantic fixation and immersive inner worlds. These experiences are often misunderstood — even by therapists. I take them seriously as windows into attachment, need, and neurodivergent inner life.

Specialty

Trauma & Grief

Relational trauma, developmental trauma, complex grief, divorce, and major life transitions. How early experience shapes your present — and how to move through it without being consumed by it.

Specialty

LGBTQ+ Identity

Gender, sexuality, and neurodivergence often intersect in complex and beautiful ways. Therapy that holds all of who you are and honors the particular stressors of navigating multiple marginalized identities.

Dr. Madrone Love

Clinical psychologist, professor, dharma practitioner.

Working as a psychologist is not just a job for me — it is my passion. I am deeply nourished by the opportunity to think and feel deeply with my clients as they heal and grow.

My practice of psychotherapy draws deeply from my Buddhist practice. I am interested in being fully present with my clients as they make their way through the vast variety of suffering that life provides. I integrate my understanding of neurodivergence into our work, whether we focus on the neurodivergence itself or other aspects of life.

I offer direct engagement, not therapeutic distance. I bring both presence and practical tools. I'll challenge you when it serves your growth and witness you when that's what's needed.
