Canvas LMS

CIIS uses Canvas as its Learning Management System. All courses are expected to have a Canvas shell, even for primarily in-person courses. Canvas is used for syllabus distribution, assignment submissions, grade posting, and announcements.

Getting Access

Your Canvas account is linked to your CIIS employee email. If you do not have access within one week of your contract being signed, contact IT support. Your course shell will be created by the Registrar and assigned to you.

Canvas Basics for ICP Instructors

  • Upload your syllabus to the Syllabus module before the first class
  • Use Announcements to communicate class changes or reminders
  • Post assignments and enable online submissions when appropriate
  • Enter grades in the Gradebook — students can view these in real time
  • SpeedGrader allows annotation and audio feedback on submissions

Library & Research Resources

The CIIS Library is located on Floor 2 of 1453 Mission St. As adjunct faculty, you have full borrowing privileges and access to all digital databases.

📖 Physical Collections

Specialized holdings in transpersonal psychology, somatic therapy, depth psychology, social justice, and world philosophies. Faculty borrowing periods are typically longer than student loans.

🔍 Digital Databases

Access to PsycINFO, PEP-Web (psychoanalytic literature), JSTOR, ProQuest, and other discipline-specific databases. Access with your CIIS credentials from anywhere.

Work with the library to place required readings on course reserve — physical or electronic. Submit reserve requests at least 3 weeks before the semester begins.

Interlibrary Loan (ILL)

Can't find a resource in CIIS collections? Request it through ILL at no cost. The library staff can usually fulfill requests within 1–2 weeks. Contact the library reference desk on Floor 2 or submit requests through the library portal.

Teaching Approaches in the ICP Tradition

ICP courses are expected to integrate CIIS's commitments to experiential learning, integral frameworks, and social justice. The following are pedagogical approaches commonly used by ICP faculty.

Experiential & Somatic Methods

  • Check-ins and closing circles to build relational container
  • Dyads and small group process work
  • Mindfulness, embodiment, and somatic awareness exercises
  • Role plays and clinical vignette discussions
  • Expressive arts as reflective practice
  • Fishbowl discussions and Socratic dialogue

Social Justice Integration

  • Power, privilege, and positionality mapping exercises
  • Diverse theoretical readings centering BIPOC, LGBTQ+, and disabled voices
  • Cultural humility frameworks in clinical case discussions
  • Community-based learning and practicum reflection
  • Critical analysis of DSM categories and psychiatric power
  • Intersectionality as a lens for understanding client experience
Note: For a deeper exploration of CIIS's pedagogical philosophy, visit the CIIS Approach page.
