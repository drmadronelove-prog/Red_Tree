Getting to CIIS

1453 Mission Street is well-served by BART, Muni, and bike lanes. Here's how to get here — and where to eat once you arrive.

📍 CIIS Address: 1453 Mission Street, San Francisco, CA 94103  ·  Located in the Civic Center / SoMa neighborhood, close to City Hall and the main BART station.

Public Transit

CIIS is one of the best-connected campuses in San Francisco for public transit. We strongly encourage faculty to take public transit — parking is expensive and limited in this neighborhood.

🚇

BART

Closest Station: Civic Center / UN Plaza — approximately a 5–7 minute walk from 1453 Mission St.

BART connects San Francisco to the East Bay (Oakland, Berkeley, Fremont), the Peninsula (SFO, Millbrae), and the South Bay (San Jose). Trains run frequently on weekdays and less frequently on evenings and weekends.

  • Exit at Civic Center / UN Plaza station
  • Walk south on 7th St or east on Market St, then south on 10th/11th to Mission St
  • Or exit at 16th Street Mission for a longer, flat walk through the Mission District
5–7 min walk bart.gov
🚌

Muni (SF Municipal Railway)

Multiple Muni lines stop within a block or two of CIIS on Mission Street and nearby corridors.

  • Muni Metro: Lines J, K, L, M, N, T stop at Civic Center Station (underground), same as BART
  • Bus Lines: The 14 (Mission), 49 (Van Ness/Mission), and other routes serve nearby stops
  • Real-time info: Use the SFMTA app or 511 SF Bay to plan your trip

Clipper Card is the easiest payment method and transfers between BART and Muni are discounted within 90 minutes.

Multiple lines sfmta.com 511.org

Biking

🚲

Bike Parking

CIIS has secure bike parking available for faculty and students. Bike racks are located:

  • On the street outside 1453 Mission St (public racks)
  • In the building's designated bike storage area — ask at the front desk for access

Always lock your bike with a sturdy U-lock, even in the building's storage area.

Confirm indoor bike storage access procedure with Facilities on Floor 5.
🛴

Bay Wheels (Bike Share)

Bay Wheels bike share stations are located near CIIS, including near Civic Center and along Market Street. Electric and standard bikes available.

The area around CIIS has bike lanes on many streets, including protected lanes on Valencia Street (parallel to Mission, 1 block east).

  • Lyft / Bay Wheels app for dock locations and availability
  • Membership options available for frequent riders
baywheels.com

Parking

⚠️ A Word on Driving

The Civic Center / SoMa neighborhood is not known for easy or affordable parking. We strongly encourage BART, Muni, or biking. If you must drive, allow extra time and budget for parking costs.

🅿️

Nearby Parking Garages

Several parking garages operate within a few blocks of CIIS. Rates vary and are subject to change.

  • Civic Center Garage — 355 McAllister St (~5 min walk)
  • 1355 Market Street Garage — near Twitter/Twitter HQ (~7 min walk)
  • SF City Hall area garages — check SpotHero or ParkWhiz for current rates and availability
Current rates and recommendations from CIIS Facilities to be added here.
🚗

Street Parking & Apps

Street parking on Mission Street and nearby blocks is metered. SFpark sensors provide real-time availability in the SF MTA app.

  • Metered hours typically 9am–6pm on weekdays; some evening meters active until 10pm
  • Evening and weekend parking can be more available, especially if you teach late classes
  • SpotHero and ParkWhiz apps can help you pre-book garage spots at discounted rates
sfpark.orgspothero.com

Cafés & Restaurants Near CIIS

The Civic Center / SoMa / Lower Haight neighborhood surrounding CIIS has a diverse range of places to eat and caffeinate before or after teaching. The following are general categories and directions — specific spots change seasonally.

This section will be updated with current faculty recommendations. In the meantime, apps like Yelp, Google Maps, or The Infatuation are your best guides to current hours and quality.
☕

Coffee & Cafés

Several independent cafés are within walking distance on Mission St, Market St, and nearby side streets. Look for cafés around the Civic Center area and the start of the Mission District (16th St corridor).

  • Blue Bottle Coffee (multiple SF locations nearby)
  • Sightglass Coffee (7th St, SoMa)
  • Ritual Coffee (various locations)
  • Local independent spots on Mission St
🌮

Quick Bites & Lunch

The Mission District — just a few blocks south — is famous for its taquerias, burritos, and diverse food options. Great for lunch before an afternoon class.

  • La Taqueria (24th St, classic SF burrito)
  • Tartine Manufactory (Inner Mission)
  • Numerous Vietnamese, Salvadoran, and Burmese spots on and near Mission St
  • Civic Center Farmers Market (Wednesdays & Sundays)
🍽️

Dinner & After Class

If you teach evening classes, the Hayes Valley and Lower Haight neighborhoods (10–15 min walk) offer excellent dinner options. SoMa also has many restaurants open late.

  • Hayes Valley restaurants along Hayes St
  • Absinthe Brasserie (Hayes St, French)
  • SoMa spots on Folsom, Howard, and Brannan
  • Japanese, Korean, and Southeast Asian restaurants within easy walk

A Note on the Neighborhood

The Civic Center / SoMa area is a vibrant and complex neighborhood. It is also an area with significant unhoused populations, particularly around UN Plaza and the Civic Center BART station. CIIS's social justice commitments include approaching our neighbors with dignity and awareness.

If you have concerns about safety on your commute, particularly for evening classes, feel free to ask the Program Coordinator about resources, buddy systems, or transportation assistance options.

Rideshare & Other Options

🚕 Rideshare

Lyft and Uber both serve the CIIS area. Drop-off on Mission St is usually straightforward. Surge pricing is common during commute hours and after evening classes end.

🚶 Walking

If you live in SoMa, the Mission, Hayes Valley, or the Castro, CIIS is potentially walkable. The Civic Center area is flat and pedestrian-friendly on the main streets.

🛴 Scooters

Shared electric scooters (Lime, Spin) are frequently available near Civic Center. Check local scooter apps for docking locations.
