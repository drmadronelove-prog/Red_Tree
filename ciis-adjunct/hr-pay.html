HR & Getting Paid

Everything you need to get onboarded, set up payroll, and understand your compensation as an ICP adjunct faculty member.

📌 This page is a living document. Contact the ICP Program Coordinator or HR Operations on Floor 5 to verify current procedures and deadlines.

Getting Started: Onboarding Checklist

Before your first class session, you'll need to complete the following steps with CIIS Human Resources. HR is located on Floor 5 at 1453 Mission St.

  1. Sign your Adjunct Faculty Contract
    Your contract specifies course(s), compensation rate, and appointment dates. Review it carefully and return a signed copy to HR.
  2. Complete your W-9 / Tax Forms
    Required for all instructors. Submit to HR prior to your first pay period.
  3. Set up Direct Deposit
    Fill out the direct deposit authorization form available from HR or the CIIS employee portal.
  4. Obtain your CIIS Employee ID & Portal Access
    HR will provision your CIIS email account and access to Canvas LMS, the employee portal, and the library database.
  5. Review CIIS Employee Handbook
    Includes policies on conduct, confidentiality, accommodations, and safety procedures.
  6. Complete Required Training Modules
    Mandatory trainings may include Title IX, FERPA, and campus safety. HR will provide links.

Compensation

Pay Structure

Adjunct faculty compensation at CIIS is determined by course unit load and the current adjunct pay scale, which is subject to collective bargaining agreements. Your contract specifies your exact rate.

Placeholder: Specific pay rates, step scales, and annual adjustments will be listed here. Contact HR or the ICP Coordinator for the current adjunct salary schedule.

Pay Schedule

CIIS processes payroll on a semi-monthly basis (twice per month). Pay dates are typically the 15th and last business day of each month. The first paycheck generally arrives 2–3 weeks after your first pay period begins.

Pay Stubs & Records

Access pay stubs through the CIIS employee self-service portal. Contact HR if you need historical pay records or have discrepancies to report.
