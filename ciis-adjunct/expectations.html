Attendance & Disability Accommodations

CIIS policies on student attendance, faculty expectations, and your responsibilities when a student presents an accommodation letter.

📌 Policies on this page reflect general CIIS guidelines. Always consult the current CIIS Academic Catalog and Student Handbook, available through the Registrar's Office, for the most up-to-date language.

Attendance Policy

CIIS values full and engaged participation as a cornerstone of experiential, relational learning — particularly in the ICP program, where the classroom itself is a therapeutic training environment. Attendance expectations reflect this philosophy.

Core Principle

Regular attendance is essential to the learning community. The relational and experiential nature of ICP courses means that absences affect not only the individual student, but the cohort as a whole. Students are expected to communicate proactively when they cannot attend.

Standard Attendance Requirements

CIIS's general policy allows students to miss no more than the equivalent of two weeks of class sessions per semester without academic penalty. For a once-weekly class, this means a maximum of two absences.

Faculty may set a stricter attendance policy in their syllabus, but not a more lenient one than the CIIS minimum standard. Attendance requirements must be clearly stated in your syllabus.

What Counts as an Absence

  • Missing more than 15 minutes of the beginning of class
  • Leaving more than 15 minutes before the end of class
  • Missing more than 30 minutes from the middle of class

Two tardies or early departures of this kind may be counted as one absence at faculty discretion.

Consequences of Excessive Absence

  • Reduction in participation or attendance grade component
  • Required meeting with faculty to discuss continuation in the course
  • Faculty referral to the Program Coordinator or Academic Affairs
  • In severe cases, a grade of W (Withdrawal) or NC (No Credit)

Faculty Responsibilities

  • Take attendance every session and maintain records
  • Reach out to students after a first unexcused absence
  • Document patterns and consult the Program Coordinator when concerned
  • Apply your stated attendance policy consistently and fairly
Tip: In ICP, many students are navigating personal healing processes alongside their studies. A compassionate, direct conversation is often more effective than policy enforcement alone.

Excused Absences & Make-Up Work

🏥 Medical & Mental Health

Students may submit documentation from a healthcare provider for medical absences. CIIS encourages faculty to respond with flexibility and care while maintaining program integrity.

⚰️ Bereavement

CIIS supports students during bereavement. Faculty should work with the Program Coordinator to arrange reasonable accommodations and extensions when a student experiences loss.

🌿 Religious Observance

Students are entitled to reasonable accommodations for religious observances. When notified in advance, faculty should provide alternate arrangements for missed content or assignments.

Make-Up Options

When a student has an excused absence, consider these make-up options:

  • Reflective journal entry on the missed topic or reading
  • Office hours conversation covering missed discussion
  • Peer consultation with a classmate who attended
  • Supplemental reading or written reflection

Make-up assignments should not simply replicate what was missed but invite the student to engage with the material in a meaningful way.

Disability Accommodations

CIIS is committed to providing equal access to education for all students. The CIIS Disability Services office coordinates accommodations for students with documented disabilities under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

When a Student Presents an Accommodation Letter

If a student gives you an official accommodation letter from Disability Services, you are legally and ethically required to implement the approved accommodations. Review the letter carefully and reach out to Disability Services if you have questions about how to implement any accommodation in your course context.

Common Accommodations in ICP Courses

  • Extended time on assignments: Typically 1.5× or 2× standard deadline. Agree on specific alternate due dates with the student.
  • Reduced attendance requirements: For students with chronic health conditions; coordinate with Disability Services on what is educationally feasible.
  • Recording permission: Some students have the right to audio-record lectures for their own use.
  • Alternative formats: Printed materials in large font, electronic text, or other accessible formats.
  • Flexible seating or movement: Some students need to stand, move, or sit in a particular location.
  • Reduced stimulation: Accommodations related to sensory sensitivities may affect room setup or activity design.

Confidentiality & Disclosure

Students are not required to disclose the nature of their disability to you. The accommodation letter is the official document — it specifies what is needed without requiring diagnostic disclosure.

Do not ask students to explain or justify their disability. Do not disclose a student's accommodation status to others in the class.

Classroom Climate & Inclusion

Beyond formal accommodations, ICP faculty are encouraged to design courses with universal accessibility in mind:

  • Offer multiple ways for students to demonstrate learning
  • Provide advance notice of course readings and materials
  • Check in with students about their access needs at the start of the semester
  • Design activities that allow for different levels of physical participation

Student Support Referrals

When a student is struggling — academically, emotionally, or personally — you do not have to navigate it alone. CIIS has multiple support structures to help students succeed.

🧠 Student Counseling

CIIS provides counseling services for enrolled students. If you are concerned about a student's wellbeing, encourage them to access these services and consult the Program Coordinator.
